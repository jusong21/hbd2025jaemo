Phonon-Mediated Cake Resonance and Birthday Wish Propagation in Layered Lattice Systems

Juhee Song1 & Jaemo's Birthday Research Collective2

1Department of Computational Celebrations, Institute for Birthday Physics

2Institute for Advanced Condensed Matter Physics and Birthday Celebrations

Abstract: We investigate the novel phenomenon of phonon-mediated cake resonance (PMCR) within layered lattice systems composed of alternating strata of sponge cake and buttercream frosting. Our study elucidates the quantum mechanical propagation of birthday wishes encoded as vibrational wavepackets through these strata. Utilizing a bespoke framework combining confectionery phononics and wish-transmission theory, we derive fundamental equations governing cake resonance frequencies, wish coherence lengths, and frosting-induced decoherence factors. Experimental pseudo-data reveal surprising correlations between cake resonance sharpness and the exponential amplification of wish propagation fidelity.
